Shri Shri Aswakranta Temple

Shri Shri Aswakranta Temple is a significant Hindu temple located in the state of Assam, India. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and holds great historical and religious importance. The temple is situated on the northern banks of the Brahmaputra River, near the town of North Guwahati. Legend and Mythology The name "Aswakranta" comes from the Assamese words "Ashwa" meaning horse and "Kranta" meaning ascended. According to Hindu mythology, the temple's name is derived from a legend associated with Lord Krishna. It is believed that this is the place where Lord Krishna rested while searching for his stolen horse, hence the name Aswakranta. Another legend suggests that the temple marks the spot where Krishna's charioteer, Arjuna, took rest after the great battle of Mahabharata. The temple is thus associated with divine episodes from the epic, adding to its spiritual significance. Architecture and Structure The temple complex is notable for its traditional Assamese architecture, characterized by the use of bricks and wood. The sanctum sanctorum houses the main deity, a stone idol of Lord Vishnu, in the form of Anantasayana Vishnu, depicting him reclining on the serpent Ananta. This unique depiction is not commonly found in other temples, making it a distinctive feature of the Shri Shri Aswakranta Temple. The temple complex also includes other shrines dedicated to various Hindu deities. The beautiful carvings and sculptures on the temple walls and pillars reflect the rich artistic heritage of the region. Festivals and Celebrations The temple is a vibrant center of cultural and religious activities. The most significant festival celebrated here is Janmashtami, the birthday of Lord Krishna, which draws thousands of devotees from across Assam and neighboring states. Other important festivals include Vishnu Yatra and Durga Puja, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Accessibility and Surroundings To reach the temple, devotees often take a ferry ride across the Brahmaputra River from Guwahati, adding a serene and scenic aspect to the pilgrimage. The journey to the temple, with the majestic river and lush green surroundings, provides a peaceful experience for visitors. Importance and Significance Shri Shri Aswakranta Temple is not only a place of worship but also a site of historical and archaeological importance. It serves as a reminder of the ancient cultural and religious traditions of Assam. The temple attracts tourists, historians, and devotees alike, offering a blend of spiritual and historical experiences. Overall, the Shri Shri Aswakranta Temple stands as a testament to Assam's rich religious heritage, architectural beauty, and cultural traditions. It continues to be a cherished destination for those seeking spiritual solace and a deeper connection with the divine.
